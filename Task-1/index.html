Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Legendary Indian Cricketer

Captain, Wicket-Keeper, and Icon

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as MS Dhoni, is a former Indian international cricketer and one of the most successful captains in the history of Indian cricket.

Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, Dhoni rose to fame with his aggressive batting style and outstanding wicket-keeping skills. He made his international debut in 2004 and quickly became an integral part of the team.

Dhoni captained the Indian cricket team to victory in multiple major tournaments including the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013. Under his leadership, India became the number one Test team in the world.

Besides his cricketing achievements, Dhoni is known for his calm and composed demeanor under pressure, earning him the nickname "Captain Cool." Off the field, he is admired for his humility, leadership qualities, and philanthropy work.

He retired from international cricket in 2020 but continues to play in the Indian Premier League (IPL), representing the Chennai Super Kings as captain and mentor.
